# LinkedIn Post — Project 001A: MNDWI Water Mapping, Prayagraj (2024–2026)

> Attach all 4 images in this order: 3‑Year Grid first, then 2024, 2025, 2026 individual panels.

I mapped the Sangam from space, three years running. Here's what changed. 🛰️

📍 **Prayagraj | Ganga‑Yamuna Confluence** | 25.4358°N, 81.8463°E
📅 **2024 – 2026** | Pre‑monsoon vs post‑monsoon
☁️ **Strict <5 % cloud filter** on every scene

**PRE‑MONSOON BASELINE (May, year on year):**
- 2024 → 9.37 sq km
- 2025 → 3.08 sq km
- 2026 → 0.87 sq km

That’s a **90.7 % drop** in pre‑monsoon surface water at this AOI over three years.

**POST‑MONSOON / ONSET RESPONSE:**
- 2024 (Oct): 9.29 sq km (‑0.82 % vs pre)
- 2025 (Oct): 8.35 sq km (+170.65 % vs pre)
- 2026 (Jun 1‑24, onset only): 3.57 sq km (+308.88 % vs pre — partial reading, not yet at seasonal peak)

**Pattern:** The summer baseline keeps getting drier, but the monsoon response gets sharper. Each year the river recovers from a lower starting point, and recovers harder.

**Scope note:** This is one 5 km‑radius AOI over three monsoon cycles – a real, measured pattern, not a 30‑year climate trend. Worth tracking further, not yet a conclusion.

---
**Method:** MNDWI (Green‑SWIR1)/(Green+SWIR1) on Sentinel‑2 L2A
**Tools:** Google Earth Engine + QGIS 3.34
**Resolution:** 10 m | fully open data, fully open tools
